Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to register a company in Hong Kong?

+

With AIcountant, company registration typically takes 1-3 business days. Our streamlined digital process and close relationship with the Companies Registry ensure quick processing. Once we receive all required documents, we can often complete the registration within 24 hours.

What documents do I need to register a company?

+

For company registration in Hong Kong, you'll need: (1) Copy of passport or HKID for all directors and shareholders, (2) Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement within 3 months), (3) Proposed company name, and (4) Business nature description. For non-residents, additional documents may be required.

Do I need a physical office address in Hong Kong?

+

Yes, every Hong Kong company must have a registered office address. However, it doesn't need to be a physical office you work from. AIcountant provides registered office address services starting from HK$199/month, which satisfies legal requirements while you work from anywhere.

What are the annual compliance requirements for a HK company?

+

Hong Kong companies must: (1) File Annual Return with Companies Registry (within 42 days of anniversary), (2) Renew Business Registration Certificate, (3) Hold Annual General Meeting, (4) Maintain proper accounting records, and (5) File Profits Tax Return. Our company secretary service handles all of these for you.

Can foreigners own 100% of a Hong Kong company?

+

Yes! Hong Kong allows 100% foreign ownership of companies. There are no restrictions on nationality for directors or shareholders. You don't need to be a Hong Kong resident to incorporate a company here. However, you will need at least one director (can be the same person as shareholder).

What taxes does a Hong Kong company need to pay?

+

Hong Kong has a territorial tax system with competitive rates: Profits Tax (8.25% for first HK$2M, 16.5% thereafter for corporations), No VAT/GST, No capital gains tax, No dividend tax, No withholding tax on dividends. Only profits derived from Hong Kong are taxable.
